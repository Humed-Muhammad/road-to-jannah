### The Type of Socks and Shoes

This section will briefly go over the type of socks and shoes that are permissible to wipe over, as there is great confusion among the average Muslim like us. We went over various scholarly opinions and gathered each opinion to present them in your hand.

Before we continue we wanted you our readers to know the difference of opinion is mostly centered on the type of socks, not the shoes.

**Regarding the Shoes**

The only requirement for wiping over the shoe is that the shoes must cover the ankle completely. In this, we can continue with the issues of the socks.

**Regarding the Socks**

The above rule also applies to the socks, the socks must cover the ankles completely too. Now the question remains what type of socks means is the socks must be a tick or can we use thin socks also?

There are two major scholarly opinions regarding this issue, one says the socks must be thick enough that the skin of the person is not visible under it and this is the majority opinion. 

The second opinion says that any type of socks is permissible given that it covers the ankles.

### List of opinions

According to **Shaykh Ibn Baaz**, one of the requirements for it to be acceptable to wipe over socks is that they must be thick and provide proper coverage. If the socks are thin, it is not allowed to wipe over them. This is because in such a case, the foot is considered as being uncovered.

**From Fataawa ash-Shaykh Ibn Baaz (10/110)**

According to **An-Nawawi**, it is acceptable to wipe over socks, even if they are thin. This is based on narrations from companions such as 'Umar, 'Ali, Abu Yoosuf, Muhammad, Ishaaq, and Dawood - may Allah be pleased with them.

But overall regarding the permissibility of thin socks the majority of the scholar agreed upon it is not permissible to wipe over thin socks.

In conclusion, most scholars believe that wiping over thin socks is not permitted and that permission to do so is restricted to thick socks. Ultimately, Allah knows best.

## Time period

Based on the genuine Sunnah, there is a specific time frame for wiping over socks. For individuals who are not traveling, this duration is one day and one night **(24 hrs)**. However, for those who are traveling, it is extended to three days and nights **(72 hrs)**.

**Al-Tirmidhi (95), Abu Dawood (157) and Ibn Majaah (553)**

It is widely accepted among scholars that the period for wiping over socks commences after the first time a person wipes over them following the break of their wudoo'. It should be noted that this period does not start from when the socks are first put on. To illustrate, if an individual performs wudoo' for Fajr prayer and wears socks, subsequently breaks their wudoo' at 9 a.m. without reperforming it, and then does wudoo' again at 12 p.m., the wiping period begins at 12 p.m. and lasts for a duration of 24 hours, encompassing one day and one night.

## Do Removing the Socks Nullify the Wudoo

Taking off your socks while having wudoo will not invalidate it. However, it is important to note that if you break your wudoo while not wearing socks, you will not be able to wipe over them and will need to perform a new wudoo by washing your feet. After completing the original wudoo, you may put your socks or shoes back on and continue wiping over them.
